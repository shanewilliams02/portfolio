Redesign

Role: UX Designer
Duration: 12 Weeks

Introduction

HelloNeighbor is an in-home elder care startup created to help customers find, schedule, and purchase in-home care for themselves or their loved ones online. I was brought on to redesign HelloNeighbor’s landing page and design a prototype of the company’s booking interface.

Problem

The question I was brought on to answer was, I was brought on to answer was, how can we design a simple, straight-forward solution that can be used by older, technologically literate, but not necessarily tech-savvy users to solve a complex challenge tied to even more complex emotions?

Solution

View Full Prototype View All Project Assets

Because booking in-home care for loved ones is relatively rare and research done throughout the project showed that HelloNeighbor’s potential customers tended to be less tech-savvy and older users, I updated the presentation to focus on usability, accessibility, transparency, and trust.

The landing page was designed to quickly and easily establish exactly how HelloNeighbor works while using branding elements to convey a sense of trust and professionalism while at the same time being open and welcoming.

I designed the HelloNeighbor interface to let users quickly schedule, customize, purchase, and review care sessions. My primary goal was to alleviate as much stress and complexity from the booking process as possible. I worked to empower users and encourage engagement in all stages of the care process. Users can monitor the care they or their loved ones receive and provide feedback on HelloNeighbor care providers.

Background

Before I was brought on, the team at HelloNeighbor had already done some market research, lo-fidelity prototyping, and created a single-page landing page site.

I obviously wanted to honor the team's work. However, I knew that to understand the problem and work toward a potential solution, I’d need to develop a thorough understanding of the in-home care space, the competitive landscape, and most importantly, the challenges faced by both those seeking and providing care.

Stakeholder Interviews

I started my research by conducting a series of interviews with HelloNeighbor’s principal stakeholders to get a firm grasp on their vision for the company, their current understanding of the market, and their work before bringing me on. I spoke with HelloNeighbor’s CEO, one of their investors, and the front-end developer who designed the original HelloNeighbor landing page.

Based on how the service works, the HelloNeighbor team knew that there would be two primary sets of users on the platform: those seeking care and HelloNeighbor-employed care providers. They also understood the critical role design would play in building and reinforcing trust.

They had a picture of their users as older and less tech-savvy. I needed to perform research to either confirm or debunk that assumption. The team was also unaware of the other players were in the market. So that’s where I turned next.

Key Stakeholder Interview Findings

Business Goals / Challenges

  • Create a platform for older adults to find in-home care and increase their quality of life.

  • Effectively communicate company’s value proposition to clients and prospective employees.

  • The company needs further demographic and competitive research to assist in creation of HelloNeighbor’s brand identity.

Design Goals / Challenges

  • Design a usable platform for care seekers and care providers can use to fulfill their goals.

  • Design for a wide range of physical and cognitive abilities as well as levels of tech-savviness.

  • The company wants customers to trust them with their loved ones. The design must establish and reinforce this sense of trust.

Competitive Analysis

To gain a thorough understanding of the in-home care space, I used Jaime Levy’s Competitive Analysis Matrix to audit the marketing sites and web applications of three major direct competitors: Honor, HomeTeamCare, and Carelinx. I was particularly interested in how these sites conveyed their value proposition, their onboarding processes, as well as how they conveyed professionalism and established trust through design.

Then, I created a SWOT analysis of these companies to identify what opportunities HelloNeighbor could take advantage of to establish itself in the marketplace.

I also curated a list of companies with track records of success in designing for our most vulnerable moments to serve as design inspiration, including Airbnb, Uber, and Lyft.

User Surveys

To better understand the needs and challenges of care seekers and care providers I designed surveys to identify how people were already providing or finding care for their loved ones and how they coordinated care with their family members. I also wanted an overview of potential users’ technological behavior and their digital wardrobe.

From professional care providers, I was eager to find out how they were matched with clients, any challenges they encountered in their work, and what methods and tools they currently used to keep track of their various clients and appointments.

Key Survey Findings

Business Goals / Challenges

  • Only 5% of participants had used online resources to find care in the past. Word-of-mouth referral was the most common form of finding care providers.

  • While the respondents ranged in age, 93% were 40 years old or older.

  • 80% of respondents used their smartphones for online browsing (shopping, banking, services)

Design Goals / Challenges

  • Educating users on how the exactly the platform works and how it differs from traditional in-home care models would be critical.

  • Following accessibility/usability best practices would be key to project success.

  • HelloNeighbor would require a responsive platform.

User Interviews and Personas

I conducted interviews with several families who were caring for their loved ones as well as some professional care providers. These conversations were invaluable in fleshing out the data and a better understanding of HelloNeighbor’s users and their needs. The interviews real value to the process was in highlighting the variety of challenges people encounter in finding and providing elder care.

I created two personas for each user group from the combined survey and interview data. I used the personas as reminders of the goals and pain points of our potential users throughout the remainder of the process to challenge and validate my design decisions.
